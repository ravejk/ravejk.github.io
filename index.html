Raven is an experienced UX Strategist and Product Designer living in Brooklyn.

She currently works at The New York Times. Among her work there she led the design of a proprietary analytics tool called Stela, which won a Publisher's Award. She also led the redesign of the Best Sellers list for the first time in a decade and a half, created a new home for NYTimes Podcasts, helped evolve the Times' first responsive framework (example: T Magazine), and was part of the small new products discovery team that identified an opportuntity for the newly launched NYT Watching.

Her past work includes research, prototyping and design for the Singaporean Government's online work permit process, ICANN.org, map-based historical photo exploration tool OldSF.org, Pitney Bowes' Canadian mailing offerings, Priceline's post-purchase and account experience, and Amplify's K-2 lesson planning tools. She also taught UX at General Assembly and wrote the curriculum for DesignLab's activity-based UX course. She has also mentored DesignLab students through this course and another on UX Strategy and Research.
